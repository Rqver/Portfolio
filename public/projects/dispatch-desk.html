About Dispatch Desk

Dispatch Desk is split into two separate projects; The Newsroom, which is responsible for generating stories, and The Frontend.

The newsroom makes use of frequent web-scraping and polling of RSS feeds listening for press releases/announcements. It grabs all content from press releases including any images, and passes it to a cheap, light-weight LLM whose only task is deciding if the story is "newsworthy", and if so, coming up with a title, tagline, and categories for the story.

The content of the press release is then passed to a larger, more expensive LLM expensive LLM that's responsible for writing the article. If the release came with an image, the written story is immediately published to the CMS. If the release didn't include an image, the title & tagline are passed to an embeddings model, which generates vectors of the title/tagline. These vectors are then passed to the database of stock/file photos, where an appropriate picture is selected, before being published.

Once the story has been published, a list of the most recent stories are passed to an LLM, along with extra context about the various slots on the page. This models task is to select which stories go in what positions on the home page.

The frontend is built to be as fast as possible and fully responsive. The home page is rendered server-side on updates from the CMS, and the entire site supports light & dark mode.

Why

I have an interest in the news media and wanted to explore how AI can and can't be used effectively in this space; There are examples of both in this project.

Dispatch Desk excels at "editorializing" and publishing stories from press releases extremely quickly. The process of press release being issued -> story live on Dispatch Desk almost always takes less than 60 seconds. However, these stories are always of a lesser quality to the real NZ Media outlets, who take the time to gather more information than what is provided in the release. AI is not capable of this (yet?), however, that additional information might not always be necessary.

I think Business Desk has implemented AI-Written "stories" really well given these constraints. They use AI to write stories on/summarize announcements & updates from the NZX. AI is capable of doing these small stories that don't need lots of extra context, and Humans and Journalists can spend their time on more in-depth reporting that AI isn't capable of.

Dispatch Desk also does well at laying out it's home page. The LLM is given context about when the most recent stories were published and the title & tagline of those stories, as well as what importance and style each spot on the home page has. I can't really fault the decisions it makes on home page layout, and I would be interested to see some testing done by a media outlet of this concept.
